### Context for SCV

Santa Clarita Valley is Tataviam homeland. Chumash territory was coastal (Malibu north to San Luis Obispo).
SCV connections are real but contextual/historical:
- Father Garcés (1776) encountered both Tataviam and coastal trade networks in the region
- 1824 Chumash revolt at Mission Santa Barbara sent refugees through the region
- Mission-era interactions created documented cultural overlap

## Chumash Audit — April 15, 2026

### Key Facts
- Primary federally recognized tribe: Santa Ynez Band of Chumash Indians
- Official website: https://chumash.gov/
- Wikidata QID: Q24251468 (Chumash people)
- Other active groups: Northern Chumash Tribal Council (non-profit, non-federally recognized) — https://northernchumash.org/

### Territorial Context for SCV
SCV is primarily Tataviam territory. Chumash territory was coastal — Malibu north to San Luis Obispo.
Chumash connections to SCV are contextual/historical, not territorial:
- Spanish missions created overlapping networks
- 1824 Chumash Revolt sent refugees through inland routes
- Father Garcés traveled through both territories in 1776
- Broader California Indigenous trade networks

Any Chumash references in SCVHistory are contextual, not core territorial content.
Do not create a separate Chumash pre-contact era — keep Tataviam-focused for SCV accuracy.
